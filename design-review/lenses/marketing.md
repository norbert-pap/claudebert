# Marketing / Landing Page Lens

Apply when reviewing marketing sites, landing pages, product homepages, or sales pages.

## Evaluation Criteria

- **Above the fold matters.** Hero communicates what this is in 3 seconds?
- **Product-grounded moment present?** At least 1-2 places where the actual product is the visual, not stock photos or abstract illustrations
- **Copy leading with outcomes or listing features?** Outcomes win. Features are what you build; outcomes are what the user gets
- **Twist visible?** Is the brand's unique visual element present and recognizable?
- **Social proof present and credible?** Testimonials, logos, case studies, metrics — essential, not optional
- **The "only" test:** would this page look identical with a different product name swapped in? If yes, the brand has no identity here
- **Each section is a mini-design of its own,** with rhythm across the page. Scroll should feel like a curated sequence, not a wall of content
- **Clarity over cleverness.** If the headline is clever but the user doesn't know what you sell, the headline failed

## Lens-Specific Provocations

- "Read me the hero headline. Now tell me: what does this product actually DO for someone? If the headline doesn't answer that, it's decoration."
- "Where's the social proof? If there isn't any, why should a stranger trust this page?"
- "Scroll the page top to bottom. Does each section feel like a deliberate beat in a story, or does it blur into a scroll?"
- "What's the single CTA on this page? If there are 3+ competing CTAs, the hierarchy is broken."
- "Show me the pricing page. Does each plan say who it's for? Does the copy sell benefits or list features? Is there a 'Start for free' instead of 'Sign up'?"
- "Where are the testimonials? Are they specific ('cut onboarding time by 40%') or generic ('love this product!')? Do they appear at the decision points — pricing, checkout, feature comparison?"
- "What are the top 3 objections a prospect has before buying? Are they answered visibly near the CTA, or buried on a FAQ page?"
- "Show me the hero for 5 seconds. What does this site do? Do I trust it? If I can't answer both, no CTA optimization will help."
