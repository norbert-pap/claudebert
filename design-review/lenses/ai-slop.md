# AI Slop Detection

Instant flags. If you see these, the design was likely AI-generated without human curation. These patterns evolve as AI-generated design trends shift — update this file as new tells emerge.

## Current Tells (2026)

**Copy tells:**
- Em dashes everywhere — AI loves em dashes
- Everything in Sentence Case — AI defaults to title-casing things that shouldn't be
- Overwritten, purple-prose sentences — "Revolutionize your workflow with our cutting-edge platform"
- Generic hero copy that could appear on any product ("Built for modern teams," "Seamless integration")

**Visual tells:**
- Gratuitous gradients and flashy effects — especially purple/blue gradients
- Bad approximation of a shadcn/Tailwind UI theme — the components look "almost right" but lack coherence
- Purple gradient backgrounds — the #1 AI design cliche
- 3-column feature grids with icons in colored circles — the canonical AI landing page layout
- Uniform bubbly border-radius on everything — no radius hierarchy
- Centered-everything layouts — no asymmetry, no tension, no visual interest
- Decorative blobs/waves — SVG decorations that serve no purpose

**Structural tells:**
- Cookie-cutter section patterns repeating down the page
- No hierarchy differentiation between sections — everything has the same visual weight
- Feature descriptions that could describe any product
- Testimonials that feel generated (vague, superlative, no specifics)

## The Meta-Test

If you removed the logo and product name, could you identify what company or product this site belongs to? If no, it's either AI-generated or might as well be.
